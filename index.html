What is conjugacy in the first place:

When a bunch of vectors are mutually conjugate with each other, the intuitive or basic level understanding is that those vectors are linearly independent and can potentially form a basis.

The two expressions are equal mathematically, but computationally, they may be different in presence of floating point errors.

The recursive formulation where the new residual is calculated based on the old residual will deviate from the definition because of floating point errors sooner or later depending on the precision of the arithematic.

And more importantly depending on the matrix A we may not arrive at the solution x after calculating n directions, because of floating point errors the directions won't be perfectly A-orthogonal.

We have to compute the residual through definition once every m iterations to avoid convergence issues

Concluding Notes:

Note that residuals are the steepest directions one can move on the convex shape.

Conjugate Gradient algorithm (CG) is basically gradient descent algorithm where the conjugacy among the directions is preserved unlike the traditional gradient descent where we directly use the residuals as directions.
